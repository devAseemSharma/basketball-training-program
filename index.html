Elite Basketball Training Program

Comprehensive conditioning and skills development designed to rebuild your athletic foundation after a layoff

Program Overview

A scientifically-designed approach to rebuild your basketball performance through progressive conditioning and skill development

Phased Approach

Start conservatively and build intensity gradually. Our program combines strength training, agility work, and basketball-specific practice to restore peak performance safely.

Consistent Schedule

Maintain consistency with 5-6 training days per week, integrating at least one rest day for optimal recovery and adaptation.

Injury Prevention

Thorough warm-ups and progressive overload reduce injury risk while protecting joints from overuse during your comeback.

Warm-Up & Mobility

Prime your muscles, joints, and nervous system with our comprehensive 15-minute routine

General Warm-Up
  • Light Cardio
    5 minutes of light jogging, skipping, or cycling to raise core temperature and heart rate, preparing your cardiovascular system for intense activity.
Dynamic Mobility Drills
  • Leg Swings & Walking Lunges
    Leg swings (front/back & side-to-side), walking lunges with torso twist, high knees, butt kicks and arm circles to mobilize hips, hamstrings, quads and shoulders.
  • Lateral Movements
    Lateral lunges and ankle rocks to improve hip and ankle mobility, essential for basketball's multi-directional movements.
Activation & Neuromuscular Prep
  • Glute Activation
    Glute bridges, monster walks, or lateral band walks to activate the gluteus medius and prepare your posterior chain.
  • Core Activation
    Planks or bird-dogs for core activation, creating a stable foundation for all basketball movements.
  • Movement Prep
    Short accelerations or defensive slides across the court to ready your nervous system for explosive movements.

Weekly Training Schedule

A comprehensive 7-day program balancing strength, conditioning, skills, and recovery

Monday - Strength & Shooting
  • Squats (3×8-12)
    Controlled squats focusing on hip, knee and ankle mobility. Keep knees tracking over toes and maintain neutral spine.
    Learn Technique
  • Glute Bridges (3×12-15)
    Lift hips until torso forms straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze glutes and hold briefly.
    Learn Technique
  • Terminal Knee Extension (3×10 each leg)
    Loop resistance band behind knee, step back for tension, bend knee slightly then straighten against band.
    Learn Technique
  • Shooting Practice
    Start with close-range form shots (20 makes), progress to mid-range and free throws. Focus on mechanics.
Tuesday - Conditioning & Skills
  • Dynamic Warm-Up (10-15 min)
    Leg swings, high knees, lateral lunges, ankle mobility drills and arm circles to prepare joints and muscles.
    Learn More
  • Shuttle Runs (3-5 sets)
    Half-court "suicide" sprints to each line and back, rest 1-2 minutes between sets.
  • Defensive Slides (5-6 sets)
    Low stance lateral shuffles across court for 20 seconds, rest 40 seconds between sets.
  • Ball-Handling Practice
    Stationary dribbles (crossovers, between-legs, behind-back) then dribble-drive moves while fatigued.
Wednesday - Strength & Skills
  • Romanian Deadlifts (3×8-10)
    Strengthen hamstrings and glutes while maintaining neutral spine throughout movement.
  • Lunges (2-3×8 each leg)
    Forward and lateral lunges keeping front knee over toes and torso upright.
  • Upper Body Strength
    Push-ups or bench press (3×8-12) paired with rows or pull-ups (3×10) for balanced development.
  • Shooting & Post Moves
    Mix mid-range shots with three-pointers, practice basic post moves (drop-step, hook shot).
Thursday - Conditioning & Recovery
  • Low-Impact Cardio (15-20 min)
    Bike, elliptical, or pool work to build endurance while reducing stress on knees and ankles.
  • Agility Ladder & Cone Drills
    Quick-feet patterns and change-of-direction drills for 15-20 minutes.
  • Mobility & Stretching
    Foam rolling and static stretches for calves, hamstrings, quads and hip flexors.
Friday - Strength & Advanced Skills
  • Full-Body Strength Circuit
    Repeat Monday's strength routine, increasing weight or reps. Include calf raises (3×20) with slow eccentric lowering.
  • Explosive Drills
    Low-volume plyometrics like pogo hops or low box jumps (2-3×5).
  • Advanced Skills
    Jab-steps, rip-through moves, and pull-up jumpers off the dribble.
Saturday - Basketball Day
  • Extended Court Session (90-120 min)
    Combine shooting drills (100+ shots), dribbling, footwork and scrimmage for realistic game application.
  • Jump Rope & Plyometrics
    If feeling strong, include jump rope intervals and low box jumps or lateral bounds.
Sunday - Rest & Recovery
  • Active Recovery
    Leisurely walk, gentle yoga, or light cycling to promote blood flow.
  • Sleep & Hydration
    Aim for 8+ hours of sleep, maintain hydration, and prepare meals for the upcoming week.

Nutrition Guidelines

Vegetarian-focused nutrition to fuel your training, muscle growth, and recovery

Protein (1.2-2.0g/kg/day)

Sources: Milk, yogurt, paneer, tofu, lentils, beans, chickpeas, nuts, and seeds. Distribute evenly across meals for optimal muscle protein synthesis.

Carbohydrates (5-7g/kg/day)

Sources: Whole grains, rice, oats, fruits, and starchy vegetables. Use easily digestible carbs (banana) before workouts for quick energy.

Hydration & Timing

Drink 2.5-3L daily, increase on training days. Post-workout: ~0.25g/kg fast-absorbing protein plus carbohydrates within 30 minutes.
